A Brief History

The popular and widely-known game of Chess can be traced back to its origins in 6th century India. In history,
Chess has been known as not just a game, but also a tool of military strategy. As trade transpired, the game
of Chess began to transcend cultures and many variations of the game developed. In China, Chess pieces were
placed at the intersections of board squares rather than inside them. Mongolians created larger 11x10 boards
with safe squares. Meanwhile, in Japan, captured pieces could be used by the opposing player. However, it was
in Europe that the game of Chess that we know now began to take form. The game of Chess even became part of
their education! Chess became a symbol of how different social classes performed their social roles; and Chess
pieces were reinterpreted into what they are now. As time progressed, Chess became more popular and players
proliferated. In 1997, an IBM computer named Deep Blue was the first computer to defeat the sitting Chess
champion, Garry Kasparov. Today, Chess software is capable of consistently defeating the best human players.
Chess continues to be a highly popular pastime and its players make up one of the largest communities in the world.
