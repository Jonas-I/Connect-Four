A Brief History

Connect Four is a two-player board game that got its concept from the widely known game of Tic-Tac-Toe, a three in a row pencil and paper game. The Milton Bradley Company, a company well-known for the creation of various board games and video games, created this board game in 1974. In the official game of Connect Four, there are 42 black and red checkered pieces; 21 of each color. Players each choose one of the black or red checkered pieces and take turns dropping their colored piece from the top of a vertical game board. A typical Connect Four game board contains seven columns and six rows of space for game pieces to fall straight down, taking the lowest space that is unoccupied in the column it was dropped into.
