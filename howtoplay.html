How to Play

Objective

    On an 8x8 checkerboard, the objective of the game is to target the opposing player's King and force it into a checkmate.

Pieces

    There are two sets of colored pieces: Black and White. White always starts first.
    There are 6 different pieces in Chess. All of them, except for the Knight, collide (they cannot jump over other pieces).
    Pawn: Moves can be made vertically one square forward (or two squares forward if it has not yet moved). Pawns can only capture pieces diagonally.
    Rook: Moves can be made any number of squares horizontally or vertically.
    Bishop: Moves can be made any number of squares diagonally.
    Knight: Moves can be made with no collision two squares vertically then one square horizontally, or two squares horizontally and one square vertically.
    Queen: Moves can be made any number of squares horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.
    King: Moves can only be made one square horizontally, vertically, or diagonally.

Terminology

    Check: The defending player's King is targeted by the attacking player's piece, however, the defending player can protect it by either moving the King or using other pieces to defend it.
    Checkmate: The defending player's King is targeted by the attacking player's piece and the defending player can neither move its King or use other pieces to defend it.
    Stalemate: A player is not checked and cannot move any pieces on their turn. Therefore, the game ends in a draw.
